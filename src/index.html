Admission Requirements
and Application Documents

  • Academic Requirements:

    Each institution sets its own criteria. You may need transcripts, GPA scores, and prerequisite subjects.

  • Credential Evaluation (WES):

    International diplomas should be evaluated through WES Canada.

  • Language Tests:

    English proficiency is typically required. Accepted tests include IELTS, TOEFL, and Duolingo English Test.

  • Letter of Acceptance:

    You must receive an official admission letter (LOA) from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI) to apply for a study permit.

Student Life in Canada

  • Health Insurance

    Most provinces require international students to purchase private health insurance or enroll in a university plan. Some provinces like British Columbia offer public coverage after a waiting period.

  • Housing

    Students can choose between on-campus residences and off-campus rentals. Average monthly rent ranges from CAD 600 to 1,200 depending on location and accommodation type.

  • Banking

    Opening a student bank account is simple and often comes with no monthly fees. Major banks like RBC, TD, and Scotiabank offer tailored services for newcomers.

  • Transportation

    Public transit is well-developed in most cities, with discounted student passes available. In places like Toronto and Vancouver, students rely heavily on subways, buses, and trains.

  • Campus Life

    Canadian universities offer a vibrant campus experience with student clubs, events, academic support, and multicultural communities to help you adapt and thrive.

Career Opportunities
After Graduation

  • Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)

    Graduates of eligible programs can apply for a PGWP, which allows them to work in Canada for up to 3 years. Learn more at IRCC.

  • Canadian Experience Class (CEC)

    After gaining skilled work experience in Canada, graduates may apply for permanent residence through the Express Entry system under the CEC stream.

  • Provincial Nominee Programs (PNP)

    Many provinces offer immigration pathways for graduates through PNPs. Requirements and eligible fields vary by province.

  • In-Demand Occupations

    Canada seeks skilled workers in tech, healthcare, engineering, trades, and education. Graduates in these fields may have a competitive edge for work and immigration.

FAQ

Is IELTS always required to study in Canada?

No, some institutions accept alternative tests such as TOEFL or Duolingo English Test. In some cases, proof of previous education in English may suffice.

Can I work while studying in Canada?

Yes, international students with a valid study permit can work up to 20 hours per week during academic sessions and full-time during scheduled breaks.

What if my visa application is refused?

If your study permit is refused, you can review the refusal letter, correct the issues (e.g., incomplete documentation), and reapply. Itâ€™s important to demonstrate strong ties to your home country and sufficient funds.

Where can I find scholarships for studying in Canada?

You can search for scholarships on official platforms such as ScholarshipsCanada and through specific universities or provincial programs.
